Clay Soil Discipline — Read Before You Act
Loudoun County clay drains slowly and compacts under foot traffic. Every lawn decision must account for this. Fertilizer on waterlogged clay causes runoff, not absorption. Aeration is not optional — it is the annual reset that makes everything else work.
  • Never apply fertilizer to saturated or frozen ground
  • Water deeply, infrequently — clay holds moisture longer than you think
  • Aerate in fall only — spring aeration on clay breaks hardpan before summer heat recovery
  • Mow at 3.5–4" in summer — taller grass shades clay surface, reducing crack-and-bake

Basement / Clay Soil Connection
Clay surrounding Brambleton foundations stays moisture-saturated longer than sandy or loamy soils. In summer this creates consistent vapor pressure against foundation walls and slabs — the symptom is a basement that stays humid even when the rest of the house is controlled. Position dehumidifier to draw air from the most problematic corner, typically opposite the sump pump.

October – November

Winterization Procedures

Irrigation System Blowout

  1. Schedule professional blowout by Oct 15 (before first hard freeze — Loudoun avg Nov 5)
  2. Locate and mark all zone valves and backflow preventer
  3. Turn off water supply to irrigation at main shutoff
  4. Connect compressed air to blowout port (min 20 CFM at 50 PSI)
  5. Open each zone manually, blow 30–60 seconds until no water exits heads
  6. Repeat each zone 2–3 passes until completely dry
  7. Insulate backflow preventer if exposed above ground
  8. Set controller to OFF or rain mode for winter

Outdoor Faucets / Hose Bibs

  1. Disconnect all garden hoses before Oct 15
  2. Locate interior shutoff valve for each exterior faucet
  3. Close interior shutoff valve
  4. Open exterior faucet to drain remaining water
  5. Leave exterior faucet slightly open overnight to confirm drainage
  6. Inspect for frost-free sillcock operation — if not frost-free, must shutoff
  7. Install foam faucet covers as secondary protection
  8. Store all hoses indoors

Gutters — Fall Sequence

  1. First clean: after majority of leaves fall (late Oct / early Nov)
  2. Second clean: after all leaves down (mid-to-late Nov)
  3. Check downspouts are clear — run hose from top
  4. Verify downspout extensions route water 6 ft from foundation
  5. Inspect for gutter sag, separated joints, or failed caulk
  6. Check fascia behind gutters for rot if gutters hold water
  7. Look for granules in gutters — indicates shingle wear

Plumbing Winterization

  1. Know location of main water shutoff — test it annually
  2. Insulate any exposed pipes in unheated spaces (crawl, garage)
  3. Keep garage doors closed when temps drop below 28°F
  4. If traveling: set thermostat no lower than 55°F
  5. Leave cabinet doors open under sinks on exterior walls in extreme cold
  6. Know how to shut off water at meter — tape the tool near it
  7. Check sump pump operation before freeze season

Landscape Winterization

  1. Wrap young cherry tree trunk with burlap or tree wrap (Nov)
  2. Apply anti-desiccant spray to boxwoods and broadleaf evergreens (above 40°F)
  3. Mulch tender perennials after first frost (3" depth)
  4. Cut back ornamental grasses to 6" in late fall
  5. Drain and store clay/ceramic pots — they crack if left out
  6. Store bird baths or drain and flip
  7. Apply deer repellent before first hard freeze (Nov 1 treatment)
  8. Check young magnolia root balls for heave; re-mulch if needed

Hardiplank Siding — Fall Check

  1. Inspect all caulk joints at windows, doors, and trim seams
  2. Look for cracks or chips in fiber cement panels
  3. Touch up damaged paint with Hardie-approved paint within 180 days
  4. Clear any soil or mulch contact with siding base (6" clearance min)
  5. Check that weep holes at base of siding are not clogged
  6. No pressure washing — gentle soft brush wash only
